How it works

  1. Tell us about your flight

    Fill in a short form — flight number, dates, airports, and what happened. Takes about 2 minutes.

  2. We check eligibility automatically

    Our rules engine checks your flight against EU261/2004. You get a plain-English result with the exact rule that applies, usually within 2 minutes.

  3. Review your claim letter

    If eligible, we draft a formal claim letter citing the regulation and the exact amount due. You read it and approve before anything is sent.

  4. We handle the airline

    We submit your claim and chase the airline. If they reject or go silent for 28 days, we escalate to the national enforcement body.

Am I covered?

EU261 applies if any of these is true:

  • Your flight departed from an airport in the EU, Norway, or Iceland (any airline)
  • Your flight arrived at an EU/EEA airport and your carrier is EU-licensed (e.g. British Airways, Ryanair, Lufthansa)

You may be owed compensation if:

  • Your flight arrived 3 or more hours late
  • Your flight was cancelled with less than 14 days' notice
  • You were denied boarding involuntarily
What about UK flights post-Brexit?

The UK has incorporated EU261 into domestic law as UK261. Flights departing UK airports or arriving at UK airports on UK carriers are covered. We treat UK airports as in-scope.
